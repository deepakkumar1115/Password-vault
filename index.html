Zero-Cloud Dependency

Cryptographic Integrity.
Absolute Confidentiality.

Most modern password managers sync your credentials to remote cloud clusters. Even with end-to-end encryption, this exposes metadata and creates centralized vectors for zero-day exploits. Secure Vault rejects this compromise.

AES-256-GCM Encryption

Your database is encrypted locally with AES-256 in Galois/Counter Mode. GCM guarantees both confidentiality and authenticity, preventing any offline tamper attempts.

PBKDF2 Key Derivation

Instead of saving your master password, Secure Vault uses PBKDF2 with HMAC-SHA256 and thousands of hashing iterations to derive the database encryption key. This makes brute-force attacks computationally impossible.

Zero Metadata Trail

Since there are no sync operations, there are no cloud logs, no trackable access timestamps, and no leakable account creation information.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our cryptographic model, offline backups, and password security.

Secure Vault works entirely locally. Your master password goes through PBKDF2 with HMAC-SHA256 stretching to derive an encryption key. The database is encrypted right on your phone using standard AES-256-GCM. Because standard cryptography works completely offline, there's absolutely no need for internet servers.
Because Secure Vault is completely zero-knowledge, we hold no cloud backups or passwords. To protect your data, you should periodically generate an "AES-256 Encrypted Backup Block" from the settings screen. You can copy and save this block safely on a flash drive, email, or notebook, and use it to restore your vault later.
Secure Vault integrates directly with the Android Keystore system. If you enable Biometrics, a secure cryptographic subkey is kept in the phone's hardware Trusted Execution Environment (TEE) or StrongBox, allowing access only after a fingerprint match. Your real master credentials never leave secure system memory.
